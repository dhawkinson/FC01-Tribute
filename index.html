A Tribute to Bob Dylan

Highlights of a legedary life

  • 1941 - May, 4 - born in Duluth, MN (St Mary's Hospital)
    • given name: Robert Allen Zimmerman
    • parents: Abraham Zimmerman and Beatrice "Beatty" Zimmerman, (nee Stone)
  • 1947 - Zimmerman family moves to Robert's mother's home town, Hibbing MN. The family remained there through his childhood.
  • Childhood - He begins to listen to music
    • first: Blues and Country - from stations as far away as Shreveport, LA
    • later: As a teenager, Rock and Roll
    • High School - played in a few bands
      • Mostly covers of performers like Little Richard, Elvis Presley and Danny and the Juniors
      • Played so loud at one High School talent show the Principal pulled the plug
  • 1959 - September Bob moved to Minneapolis, to attend the University of Minnesota
  • 1960 - Drops out of college at the end of his first year
  • The 60s
    • 1961 - January, he moves to new York City
    • 1961 - February, begins to play clubs in Greenwich Village
    • 1961 - September, gains public recognition due to positive review by Robert Shelton of the New York Times
    • 1961 - October, signed to Columbia Records
    • 1962 - March, his first album, "Bob Dylan" released (breaks even)
    • 1962 - August, legally changes his name to Robert Dylan and signs with Albert Grossman as a manager
    • 1962 - December, first trip to the UK, first public performance of "Blowing in the Wind"
    • 1963 - May, second album "Freewheelin' Bob Dylan" introduces stream of consciousness singing with "Blowin' in the Wind" and "A Hard Rain's a-Gonna Fall"
    • 1963 - May, refuses to perform on Ed Sullivan Show rather than be censored
    • 1965 - March, goes electric with "Bringin' It All Back Home", fractures his relationsship with the folk music scene
    • 1965 - July, Newport Folk Festival, first electric public performance since high school
    • 1965 - Goes on tour, gets ground down by the pressure of it
    • 1965 - November, Dylan secretly marries Sara Lownds, a 25 year old former model. They had four children.
      • 1966 - Jesse Byron Dylan
      • 1967 - Anna Lea Dylan
      • 1968 - Samuel Isaac Abram Dylan
      • 1969 - Jakob Luke Dylan
      • ca1968 - Maria Dylan (nee Lownds), adopted by Dylan (born 1961)
    • 1966 - July, a motorcycle accident provides him an escape from the rat race. Doesn't tour again for nearly eight years
    • 1967 - October/November, Dylan returns to Nashville and produces "John Wesley Harding"
    • 1968 - January, return to public appearance at a memorial for Woodie Guthrie. Backed by the Band.
    • 1969 - Releases "Nashville Skyline", an honest Country/Western album with a duet with Johnny Cash
    • 1969 - May, appears on the first episode of the Johnny Cash Show
  • The 70s
    • 1970 - June, releases "Self-Portrait" to unfavorable review. In October, releases "New Morning", hailed as a return to the old Dylan
    • 1971 - Sees the release of songs recorded with Leon Russell, "Watching the River Flow" and a new recording of "When I Paint My Masterpiece". In November came "George Jackson", a return to protest material.
    • 1972 - Dylan signs to do the soundtrack for "Pat Garrett and Billy the Kid", the Sam Peckinpah film. The film flopped but "Knockin' on Heaven's Door" became on of Dylan's most covered songs.
    • 1973 - Sees the release of "Planet Waves" on a new label Asylum Records.
    • 1974 - January, Dylan returns to touring with the Band, drops Asylum over poor sales and returns to Columbia.
    • 1974 - September, Dylan and his wife become estranged. He records but doesn't release "Blood on the Tracks".
    • 1975 - A remastered "Blood on the Tracks" is released. "Hurricane", a tribute to Rubin "Hurricane" Carter is released as a single. The Rolling Thunder Revue tour ensues, with a cast of about 100 performers and friends.
    • 1976 - Dylan appears at The Band's farewell concert.
    • 1977 - He and Sara divorce
    • 1978 - He embarks on a year long round the world tour, performing 114 shows in Japan, the Far East, Europe and the US, to a total audience of two million.
    • 1979-81 - The Christian Era, sees the release of "Slow Train Coming", "Saved" and "Shot of Love", and his refusal while touring to play his older, secular songs.
  • The 80s
    • 1981 - With the release of "Shot of Love" he begins a return to his roots with the mixing of secular and Christian themes.
    • 1980s - Dylan met with patchy success. "Infidels" (1983) was well received. "Down In The Groove" (1988) was panned.
    • 1985 - The release of "Empire Burlesque" saw a engineering/mixing job by Arthur Baker, possibly designed to make Dylan sound a little bit more contemporary.
    • 1986 - April, Dylan tests the water with rap when he added vocals to the opening verse of "Street Rock", featured on Kurtis Blow's album Kingdom Blow.
    • 1986 - July, "Knocked Out Loaded" release. It was his first album since "Freewheelin'" (1963) to not make the the top 50.
    • 1988 - January, inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
    • 1988 - May, "Down In The Groove" release and tanks worse than "Knocked Out Loaded".
    • 1988 - The Travelling Wilburys is formed with George Harrison, Jeff Lynne, Roy Orbison, and Tom Petty to critical acclaim.
    • 1989 - Dylan finishes the decade on a high note with the release of "Oh Mercy".
  • The 90s
    • 1990 - With "Under the Red Sky", he makes an about-face from the serious "Oh Mercy". Contained several apparently simple songs. The album was dedicated to "Gabby Goo Goo", a nickname his four year old daughter with Carol Dennis. Despite a strong line-up of side men, the record sold poorly.
    • 1991 - Dylan receives a Lifetime Achievement Award at the Grammys.
    • 1992-93 - Dylan returns to his folk roots with "Good as I Been to You" (1992) and "World Gone Wrong" (1993).
    • 1994 - He records two live shows for MTV Unplugged.
    • 1994 - He quits drinking according to his longtime road manager.
    • 1997 - Dylan collapses in the middle of recording sessions and is hospialized with pericarditis. Though he nearly died, he recovered quickly and was touring again by mid year.
    • 1997 - He performed before Pope John Paul II at the World Eucharistic Conference in Bologna, Italy. The Pope treated the audience of 200,000 people to a homily based on Dylan's lyric "Blowin' in the Wind".
    • 1997 - December, U.S. President Bill Clinton presented Dylan with a Kennedy Center Honor in the East Room of the White House
    • 1999 - Dylan tours with Paul Simon, alternating headlining spots with a middle portion of the concert that features the two of them playing together.
  • The 21st Century
    • 2000 - May, He receives the Polar Music Prize, a Swedish international award founded in 1989. The prize has been called the "Nobel Prize of Music" in Sweden.
    • 2001 - March, Dylan receives his first Oscar for his song "Things Have Changed", written for the film "Wonder Boys".
    • 2001 - September 11, the release of "Love And Theft" receives critical acclaim.
    • 2003 - He returns to his gospel days with the CD project "Gotta Serve Somebody: The Gospel Songs of Bob Dylan"
    • 2003 - Sees the release of the film "Masked & Anonymous", which Dylan co-wrote with director Larry Charles under the alias Sergei Petrov. Dylan played the central character in the film, Jack Fate, alongside a cast that included Jeff Bridges, Penélope Cruz and John Goodman.
    • 2004 - October, Dylan published the first part of his autobiography, Chronicles: Volume One. It goes to Number Two on the NYT Best Seller, non-fiction list.
    • 2005 - September, "No Direction Home", Martin Scorsese's acclaimed film biography of Dylan, was first broadcast on BBC Two in the UK and PBS in the US.
    • 2006-09 - Dylan hosts a Radio Talk Show on XM Satellite Radio. The broadcasts were centered about a musical theme for the day with music and discussion.
    • 2007 - A study of US legal opinions and briefs that found his lyrics were quoted by judges and lawyers more than those of any other songwriter. Among those quoting Dylan were SCOTUS Chief Justice John Roberts and Justice Antonin Scalia, both conservatives.
    • 2009 - October, Dylan released a Christmas album, "Christmas in the Heart". Dylan's royalties from the sale of this album will benefit the charities Feeding America in the USA, Crisis in the UK, and the World Food Programme.
    • 2011 - May 24 (70th birthday), three universities, Mainz, Vienna, and Bristol invited literary critics and cultural historians to give papers on aspects of Dylan's work. Other events, including tribute bands, discussions and simple singalongs, took place around the world.
    • 2014 - February, Dylan appeared in a commercial for the Chrysler 200 car which was screened during the 2014 Super Bowl.
    • 2014 - October, Simon & Schuster published a massive 960 page, thirteen and a half pound edition of Dylan's lyrics, Lyrics: Since 1962.
    • 2015 - February, Dylan released "Shadows in the Night", featuring ten songs written between 1923 and 1963, which have been described as part of the Great American Songbook.
    • 2016 - March, it was announced that Dylan had sold an extensive archive of about 6,000 items to the George Kaiser Family Foundation and the University of Tulsa. It was reported that the sale price was "an estimated $15 million to $20 million", and the archive comprises notebooks, drafts of Dylan lyrics, recordings, and correspondence.
    • 2016 - May, Dylan released his 37th studio album, Fallen Angels, which was described as "a direct continuation of the work of 'uncovering' the Great Songbook that he began on last year’s Shadows In the Night."

I have tried, in this tribute, to paint a picture of neither a saint nor a demon. I have cherry-picked through the events of Bob Dylan's life with regard for only one goal, to paint a picture of a man with serious convictions regarding social justice, what is good and what is right, who had the courage to stand by his convictions, whatever the consequences. They are maybe not the events that he would see as the most significant. I don't know. I have never met the man. But I have listened to his music (most of it) for more than 50 years, and seen him in live concert once. I find myself quoting his lyrics, as have Supreme Court Justices, because they are relevant to life. So, I have picked the events that indicate milestones to me.

In 54 years he has released 37 albums, some to acclaim, some to scorn. Dylan has experimented with a wide variety of genres, from folk to rap. But he has never pandered to either his fan base or commercial manipulation. During his gospel tenure, he suffered tremendous abuse by the critics and his fan base. But he unflinchingly stuck to his guns and played from his convictions. He has walked out on shows, refusing to be censored. He has received accolades from Presidents and Popes. He has been honored by the music industry and academia. He has sung, produced, written (music, books and screenplays), acted, been a benefactor. He is a true Renaissance Man.
